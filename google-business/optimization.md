# Optimizing Your Google Business Profile

> **Google rewards completeness, accuracy, and activity. Here is everything you need to know to rank higher and convert more customers.**

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is often the first thing a potential customer sees when searching for your type of business. A fully filled-out, frequently updated, and actively managed profile ranks higher in local search and converts more visitors into customers than a neglected one.

{% hint style="info" %}
Google explicitly states that complete and accurate information increases your visibility in local search. Incomplete profiles rank lower, regardless of how long you have been in business.
{% endhint %}

---

## How Google Ranks Local Businesses

Google determines local search ranking based on three factors. Your optimization efforts directly influence two of them.

| Factor | What It Means | Your Control |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| **Relevance** | How well your profile matches what the customer searched for | High: categories, description, services |
| **Distance** | How close your business is to the searcher | Low: fixed by location |
| **Prominence** | Your reputation, reviews, and overall activity level | High: reviews, posts, engagement |

---

## Top 10 Optimization Priorities

The following priorities are ranked by their impact on local search visibility, based on Google's own optimization documentation.

### 1. Complete Your Business Information
Add your address, service area, phone number, business categories, and attributes like parking availability or Wi-Fi. Every empty field is a missed ranking signal.

### 2. Verify Your Business
Verification tells Google you are the legitimate owner of this listing, which increases your likelihood of appearing in search results and protects your profile from unauthorized edits.

### 3. Keep Your Hours Accurate
Update regular hours, holiday hours, and any temporary closures. Google cites accurate hours as a direct ranking and trust factor. Nothing frustrates a potential customer more than showing up when you are closed.

### 4. Respond to Every Review
Replying to reviews, positive and negative, shows you value customer feedback. Google explicitly calls this out as a ranking and trust signal. Even a brief, genuine response makes a difference.

### 5. Add Photos and Videos
Businesses with photos receive significantly more direction requests and website clicks. Show your location, team, work samples, or completed projects. Aim for at least 10 to 20 high-quality images.

### 6. Post Updates Regularly
Google Posts (updates, offers, and events) keep your profile active and signal relevance to the algorithm. Aim for at least one post per week. Consistency matters more than perfection.

### 7. Choose the Right Business Categories
Your primary category determines the majority of your ranking potential. Secondary categories help you appear for additional services.

### 8. Add Products or Services
Detailed service and product listings help Google match your profile to specific search queries and can appear directly in search results before a customer ever visits your website.

### 9. Ensure NAP Consistency
Your Name, Address, and Phone Number must match exactly across your website, social profiles, and online directories. Inconsistencies confuse Google's local algorithm and can suppress your ranking.

### 10. Monitor Your Insights
Google provides data on searches, calls, direction requests, and photo views. Review this monthly to understand what is working and where to focus your efforts.

## Non-Obvious Insight: The Category Problem

{% hint style="warning" %}
Your primary business category determines approximately **80% of your ranking potential**. Choosing the wrong one, even by a small margin, can significantly limit your visibility regardless of how well the rest of your profile is optimized.

**Examples of common category mistakes:**

| Too Generic | More Specific (Better) |
|------------|----------------------|
| Security Company | Security System Supplier |
| Marketing Agency | Internet Marketing Service |
| Plumber | Drainage Service |

Choose the category that best matches your **highest-value** service, not the broadest description of what you do.
{% endhint %}

## Quick-Start Checklist

Use this checklist to audit your current profile and identify the fastest improvements available to you.

- [ ] Business is verified with Google
- [ ] Name, address, and phone number are complete and accurate
- [ ] Primary business category matches your main service
- [ ] Secondary categories added for additional services
- [ ] Business hours are current including holidays
- [ ] Business description is written and compelling
- [ ] At least 10 high-quality photos have been uploaded
- [ ] Services or products are listed with descriptions
- [ ] All recent reviews have a response
- [ ] At least one Google Post published this week
- [ ] NAP matches your website and other online listings
- [ ] Google Insights reviewed in the last 30 days
